CCTV - Closed Circuit Television Camera

CCTV cameras are counted by the camera unit. Cameras may or may not share a cabinet with other VDS systems. Multiple cameras at a single location will be counted as individual inventory units. The entire system may consist of mounting hardware, a dedicated pole to mount the camera, hardware to connect power and data, pullboxes, conduit, controller and cabinet to house the system.

CMS - Changeable Message Sign

Changeable message signs are counted by the sign panel. Systems will include the mounting hardware or structure, data and power connections, pullboxes, conduit, and the controller and cabinet.

EMS - Extinguishable Message Sign

Extinguishable message signs are counted by the sign panel. Systems will include the mounting hardware or structure, data and power connections, pullboxes, conduit, and the controller and cabinet.

FB - Flashing Beacon

Highway Advisory Radio (HAR) broadcasts road/ travel information to the motoring public. The HAR is supported by Flashing Beacons (FB) and a fixed sign. When the FB is on (flashing yellow lights) it draws drivers attention to the sign that displays the frequency to tune to hear pertinent road information.

HAR - Highway Advisory Radio

Highway advisory radio stations are counted by the system. This includes the transmitter, antenna, pole, conduit, and controller cabinet.

RM - Ramp Meter

Ramp meters are counted at the cabinet level, similar to traffic monitoring stations. Ramp meter cabinets may also provide traffic monitoring adjacent to the ramp meter system, this is included as part of the ramp meter, not as a separate traffic monitoring station. Ramp meter systems may include multiple traffic monitoring functions, both on the ramp and adjacent lanes, metering signals, pullboxes, conduit, cabinet, and controllers.

RWIS - Roadside Weather Information System

Roadside weather information systems (RWIS) are counted by the weather station. An RWIS may consist of multiple components to detect the local weather conditions, such as visibility sensors, cameras, anemometer , rain gauge, barometers, pavement surface probe and ice sensors. More or less sensors may be included, depending on the functions needed at the location. RWIS locations may include any necessary power, conduit, pull boxes, communication infrastructure, and controller cabinet.

Signal - Intersection Traffic Signal

Traffic signals are counted by the signalized intersection that uses the signals. Even if multiple complete signal systems exist at an intersection, only one unit is counted. Traffic signals include the pole structures, signals, foundations, conduit and wiring, service enclosures, pedestrian heads, and controller cabinets.

VDS - Vehicle Detection System

Vehicle detection system,also known as detector stations or traffic monitoring stations, or are counted at the cabinet level. Each cabinet may have multiple controllers, and each controller may detect traffic in multiple lanes. In the event that a cabinet also houses a controller used with a CMS or CCTV camera, it is still considered to be the traffic monitoring stationâ€™s cabinet for inventory purposes. The traffic monitoring station includes the cabinet and controllers, along with any hardware used for vehicle detection, such as inductive loops, radar, or piezometers.

VCS - Vehicle Classification Station

Vehicle size classes are a way of classifying cars. The common North American parlance is word-based, while English-speaking European writers also use words to describe car sizes. In parts of Asia, segment letters are sometimes used.

WIM - Weigh In Motion

Weigh-in-motion or weighing in motion (WIM) devices are designed to capture and record axle weights and gross vehicle weights as vehicles drive over a measurement site. Unlike static scales, WIM systems are capable of measuring vehicles traveling at a reduced or normal traffic speed and do not require the vehicle to come to a stop. This makes the weighing process more efficient, and, in the case of commercial vehicles, allows for trucks under the weight limit to bypass static scales or inspection.
